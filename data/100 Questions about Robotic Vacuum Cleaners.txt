### Cleaning Effect Optimization

26. **What to do if the kitchen floor is heavily oiled and doesn't get clean after mopping?**

27. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto clean pet urine stains?**

28. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto handle stubborn stains like chewing gum and adhesive on the floor?**

### Hardware Faults and Alarms

41. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto handle the "Mopping System Abnormal" alarm?**

- Check if the water tank and mop are installed correctly, and if the mop motor is stuck. Clean debris and hair from the mopping module. If the alarm persists after restarting the robot, contact customer service.

42. **What to do if a "Water Tank Not Detected" alarm occurs even though the water tank is installed?**

- Check if the water tank sensor is blocked by dust or water stains. Wipe the sensor with a dry, soft cloth, reinstall the water tank, and ensure the clips are securely fastened.

43. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto resolve the "clicking" noise when the mop rotates?** **
-**Disassemble the mop head, clean any tangled hair, threads, and small debris from inside the support bracket, and check if the mop motor bearings are low on lubrication. Apply a small amount of dedicated lubricating oil (do not use too much).

44. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto handle the "Abnormal water level in cleaning chamber" alarm on automatic mop washing models?**

-**Check if the cleaning chamber is filled with clean water to the mark and if the wastewater is full. Clean the water level sensor in the cleaning chamber, refill with water, empty the wastewater, and restart.**

45. **What to do if the robot suddenly stops mopping?**

-**Check the remaining battery power. If the battery is low, it will return to the charging dock first. If the battery is sufficient, check if the mopping module is stuck or if the sensors are blocked. Clean them and try again.**

46. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto resolve the "Mop bracket not installed" alarm?**

-**Confirm that the mop bracket is fully engaged in the body's slot and that the bracket clips are not damaged. If damaged, replace the bracket, reinstall it, and restart the robot.**

47. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto unclog the water tank outlet pipe?** **
- Gently unclog the water outlet with a toothpick or thin wire, avoiding excessive force that could damage the pipe; then rinse the inside of the water tank with clean water to ensure smooth water flow.

48. **What to do if the robot mops wobbles or shifts direction during mopping?**

- Check if the mop is installed evenly, if there are any obstructions on the drive wheels, and if there is any water on the floor causing slippage. After cleaning, place the robot on a level surface and restart it.

49. **What to do if the automatic mop lifting function fails?**

- Confirm that the function is enabled in the app. Check if the mop lifting motor is stuck, clean any obstructions around the motor, and if it still doesn't work after restarting the robot, contact customer service.

50. **What to do if the "Cleaning solution concentration too high" alarm sounds?**

- Drain all the liquid from the water tank, rinse the tank several times with clean water, remix the cleaning solution and water according to the correct ratio, refill, and restart the robot.

51. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto solve the problem of black scratches left by the robot mopping?** **
- Check the mop bracket for wear and paint chips, and check for sand or hard objects trapped inside the mop. Clean the mop promptly and replace worn bracket parts.

52. **What to do if the mopping module continues to work while the robot is charging?**

- Restart the robot to check if the malfunction is caused by a system freeze. If the problem persists, restore factory settings in the app and reset the cleaning parameters.

53. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto resolve the "Wastewater tank filter clogged" alarm?**

- Remove the wastewater tank filter, rinse the surface with clean water to remove debris and stains, let it dry, and then reinstall it. Clean the filter regularly to prevent re-clogging.

54. **What to do if the mop motor doesn't turn and the robot body is overheating?**

- Immediately turn off the robot's power to prevent motor burnout. Check if the motor is jammed by debris. If it cannot clear itself, contact after-sales service for inspection and repair.

55. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto resolve the issue of the mopping function automatically shutting off after the robot crosses a threshold?** **
- Check if the threshold height exceeds the robot's adaptability range. Enable the "Resume Mopping After Obstacle Crossing" function (supported by some models). Manually assist the robot to cross the obstacle and then restart mopping.

### Consumables and Maintenance

56. **What is the lifespan of the mop head?**

- Washable mop heads should be replaced every 3-6 months under normal use. Replace every 1-2 months in pet-friendly households or areas with heavy oil stains. Disposable mop heads are for single use only and should be replaced after each use.

57. **How ‚Äã‚Äãoften should the water tank seal be replaced?**

- The seal is a wear part and should be replaced every 6-12 months. Replace immediately if it shows signs of aging, cracking, or leakage.

58. **How ‚Äã‚Äãoften should the cleaning brush of an automatic mop head washing model be cleaned?**

- Clean the brush of hair and debris each time you empty the wastewater. Rinse with clean water weekly. Replace immediately if it shows signs of wear or deformation.

59. **Does the bearing of the mopping module require maintenance?** **
- Yes, apply 1-2 drops of dedicated lubricating oil to the bearing every 3 months to prevent rust and jamming, ensuring smooth mop rotation.

60. **Can the filter be used in the mopping system?**

- No, sweeping filters and mopping system filters have different specifications and functions. Mopping filters primarily filter wastewater and require a dedicated filter. Using the wrong filter can damage the equipment.

61. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto clean the sensors in the mopping system?**

- Gently wipe the sensor surface with a dry, soft cloth or lint-free cloth. Avoid using a damp cloth or alcohol to prevent moisture damage.

62. **How ‚Äã‚Äãshould consumables be replaced in automatic dust collection + automatic mop washing models?**

- Replace the dust bag every 1-2 months, the mop every 3 months, and the filter every 1-2 months for cleaning and every 6 months for replacement. Adjust according to usage frequency.

63. **Can a broken mop holder clip be repaired?** **
- Once the clip breaks, it cannot be repaired. A genuine original bracket of the same model must be replaced. Avoid using non-original parts, as this can lead to poor mopping performance or damage to the machine.

64. **How ‚Äã‚Äãto maintain the water pump of an automatic mop washing machine?**

- When not in use for extended periods, drain the water from the cleaning chamber and water tank, and flush the water pump pipes with clean water to prevent clogging by scale and dirt. Clean with a descaling agent every 3 months.

65. **Can disposable mop cloths be washed and reused?**

- It is not recommended. Disposable mop cloths are designed for single use only. Washing them will cause them to harden, shed lint, and affect mopping performance, and may even clog the mop head.
